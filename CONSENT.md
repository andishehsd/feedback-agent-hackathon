# Research Participation & Informed Consent

**Study Title:** Can AI Give Good Feedback? A Participatory Hackathon Study of Student-designed AI Feedback Agents

**Researcher:** Mary-Jo Diepeveen
**Institution:** Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam  
**Conference Submission:** ICERI2026 (Seville, Spain, November 9-11, 2026)

---

## About This Research Study

This hackathon is part of a research study examining how students conceptualize effective AI-mediated feedback. Your participation will help researchers understand:

1. **What features of AI feedback do students prioritize** when designing feedback agents?
2. **How do students operationalize rubric anchoring** when co-designing AI feedback agents?

The findings will be presented at the ICERI2026 conference and may be published in academic journals focused on educational technology and learning sciences.

---

## What Does Participation Involve?

### If You Participate in the Hackathon (Required):

- Complete a 50-100 word science writing summary
- Rate a peer's summary using the Universal Science Writing Rubric
- Participate in team-based agent design activities
- Test another team's agent and provide feedback via GitHub Issues
- Vote for the most effective agent

**Time commitment:** 3 hours (18:00-21:00 on June 17, 2026)

### If You Consent to Research (Optional):

Your participation in the **research component** is **completely voluntary**. You can participate in the hackathon event without consenting to have your data included in the research study.

---

## What Data Will Be Collected?

If you consent to participate in the research, the following data may be collected and analyzed:

### Data Sources:

1. **Your initial writing sample** (50-100 word summary)
2. **Peer rating worksheets** you complete
3. **Pre-hack reflection responses** about what makes feedback useful
4. **Agent instruction files** your team creates (v1 and v2)
5. **GitHub Issues** you submit as feedback on other teams' agents
6. **Community vote outcomes** and your vote justification
7. **Exit reflection responses** about your experience
8. **Facilitator observation notes** (general, not individual-specific)

### What Will NOT Be Collected:

- Your name (data will be anonymized)
- Any identifying information beyond general demographics (e.g., "university student")
- Private communications between team members
- Any data created after the event ends

---

## How Will Your Data Be Used?

### Analysis:

- Researchers will analyze agent designs to identify common patterns and strategies
- Feedback artifacts (GitHub Issues, reflections) will be examined thematically
- Aggregated findings will inform understanding of student feedback literacy

### Publication:

- Results will be presented at ICERI2026 (November 2026)
- Findings may be published in peer-reviewed journals
- **All data will be anonymized** — no names or identifying information will be published
- Example quotes from agent instructions or reflections may be included, but will be de-identified

### Sharing:

- Anonymized data may be shared with other researchers upon request
- Your GitHub repository will need to be public for other students to evaluate and provide feedback on your agents
- A summary of findings will be shared with participants who provide their email

## Data Protection & GDPR Compliance

This study complies with the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and Dutch data protection laws.

### Your GDPR Rights:
- **Right to access**: Request a copy of your data
- **Right to rectification**: Correct any inaccurate data
- **Right to erasure**: Request deletion of your data (before publication)
- **Right to restrict processing**: Limit how your data is used

- Your explicit consent (freely given, specific, informed)
